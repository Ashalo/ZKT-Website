About Me

Aspiring data scientist specializing in applied machine learning, LLM evaluation systems, and interpretable data visualization. Experienced in designing modular, metric-driven experimentation pipelines, engineering domain-specific features from complex real-world datasets, and building interactive visual analytics tools that make model behavior legible to both technical and non-technical stakeholders.

I have worked extensively with probabilistic models, logistic regression, and feature engineering to surface signal from noisy data, and I am particularly interested in how evaluation frameworks shape the reliability and trustworthiness of generative AI systems. My work emphasizes rigorous experimentation, reproducibility, and measurable performance improvements through systematic iteration rather than ad-hoc tuning.

Beyond modeling, I focus on creating tools and visual systems that expose why a model behaves the way it does. This includes building dashboards, interactive visualizations, and evaluation pipelines that allow users to diagnose errors, interpret model decisions, and compare model behavior across scenarios. I am especially drawn to problems involving human behavioral data, game analytics, and environments where probabilistic reasoning and uncertainty play a central role.

My broader goal is to build machine learning systems that are not only performant, but transparent, testable, and grounded in well-designed evaluation methodology.
